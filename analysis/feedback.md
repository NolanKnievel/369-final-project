## Addressing Feedback

The main feedback from reviewers on my rough draft roughly focused on three main areas: visualization clarity, the depth of holiday analysis, and the overall organization of the paper.

- **Difficult-to-Vnterpret Visualizations**
    - Reviewrs found the map visualizations hard to interpret. They were unsure which regions of New York the maps referred to and felt I didn't provide enough context about the city's geography. Likewise, comparing multiple heatmaps side by side was challenging. In the rogugh draft, many graphs were created using samples of the full dataset in order to stay compatible with Tableau. However, this contributed to general confusion about whether the meaning of the values.

    - In the final version, I've improved clarity of the visuals by adding labels and giving more context. I also created visuals using actual counts and aggregated values rather than with samples. Ride zone maps now display chagnes relative to expected ride counts, which more clearly displays how demand shifts on holidays.

- **More analysis on other holidays**
    - Another suggestion from readers was to do a deeper analysis into more holidyas, as in my original I only really dove deep into New Year's Eve. This made broader conclusions about holidays in general less convincing.

    - In the final version, I do a deeper analysis on a range of holidays, using multiple forms of visuals for these days as well. 
- **General flow of the paper**
    - In the first draft, the structure of the paper was mostly centered around teh dataset itself and the types of questions it could asnwer. Reviewers found this structure confusing and sometimes had difficutly understanding the objective of the analysis. 

    - To address this, I reorganized the paper so that it's strucutred around my central question about holiday impact. The revised version introduces the question early, presents analysis that attempts to asnwer it, and concludes with a clear interpretation of the results. The new structure iimproves the flow of teh paper and makes it easier for readers to follow its argument.
